Research

Our team has researched some current fitness apps that lack accessibility for the disabled. The goal is to provide speech control, virtual coach service and accessibility everywhere.

Literature Review

Introduction

We have reviewed current software products that offer similar services to the ones the “VeloCity” project aims to deliver. There are many fitness tracking apps on the market, but they do not seem to fit the needs of people with disabilities. They tend to be very visual, which is not suited for blind and visually impaired. Furthermore, health tracking in these apps is created around an average person, putting everyone else out of the equation. We are here to change that for the better, focusing on specific needs of everyone. Cherishing their abilities, but being aware of their disabilities, and creating the app surround them and not a fictional person. However, some companies are trying to change that with more or less success. Our goal is to gather insights from existing apps, both pros and cons. Finally, getting the right view of what has been done, what must be fixed, as well as what innovation we have to add, to make people’s dreams a reality. Making everyone feel included and important both as individuals, and as a collective as well.

Zwift

Although Zwift does not offer full range of fitness tracking services, it is the closest thing to what our goal would be. Giving seamless sports experience with big accent on its social aspects. Zwift is bringing people together and making them lead healthier lifestyles through cycling, which is amazing. Xamarin & .NET MAUI

After discussions with our client, we decided to rebuild the app on .NET MAUI with an intuitive UI layout to reach the visually impaired convenienve. Also, with transforming from Xamarin to .NET MAUI, the renewed architecture provides higher performance, an easier way of future extension, and is simpler for developers

Speech Control

Another requirement is to enable voice control over the app. To implement speech recognition and language understanding, the Azure Cognitive Service is used as its built-in AI could accelerate the project lifecycle
